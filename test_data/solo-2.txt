People think surgery is all about the hands, but it’s actually about the data. In the heat of a procedure, I can’t stop to type notes. I talk to the room. The ASR system catches every 'scalpel,' every 'suture,' every vital sign I call out. Because of diarization, the logs clearly show it was the anesthesiologist who flagged the heart rate spike, not me. It creates a perfect, objective record of the 'why' and 'when.' It doesn’t just reduce paperwork; it saves us from the fallibility of human memory when lives are on the line.
